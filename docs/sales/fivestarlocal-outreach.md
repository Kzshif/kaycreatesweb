# FiveStar Local — outreach kit

Cold outreach for the review service. The formula that makes this convert:
**open with their actual numbers.** Before sending, spend 60 seconds on Google
Maps and fill in the two counts. Never send without them.

Swap `[bracketed]` bits. Keep emails short — practice managers skim.

## Walk-in script (highest conversion)

Print a sample table card with THEIR practice name on it (`cards.html`, two
minutes). Hand it to the practice manager:

> "This is for you — it already works. Patients scan it, rate the visit, and
> land on your Google review form. I set the whole thing up for £39 a month,
> and if your first month brings no new reviews it's free. Can I show you the
> 10-second demo?"

Leave the card either way. It has their name on it; it stays on the desk.

## Tips

- **Numbers first.** "You have 31, they have 210" outperforms every clever
  subject line. It proves you looked, and it stings a little.
- **Demo, not meeting.** Link the review page pre-filled with their name
  (`review.html?b=Their+Practice&g=THEIR_PLACE_ID`). Seeing their own name on
  it does the selling.
- **Compliance is your differentiator.** Most cheap review services gate or
  fake. Saying plainly "we never gate, incentivise, or write reviews" wins the
  cautious healthcare buyer.
- **Two touches, then stop.**
